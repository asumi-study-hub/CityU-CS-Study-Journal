# Overview: Complete Project Management Notes Reorganized by Process Groups

## **INITIATING PROCESS GROUP**

### **Project Integration Management**
- **Process:** Develop Project Charter
- **What to do:** Working with stakeholders to create the document that formally authorizes a project
- **Tools/Techniques:**
  - Expert judgment
  - SWOT analysis (analyzing Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
  - Meetings with stakeholders
- **Inputs:**
  - Business case
  - Project statement of work
  - Enterprise environmental factors
  - Organizational process assets
- **Outputs:**
  - Project charter (formally recognizes project existence)
  - Assumption log

### **Project Stakeholder Management**
- **Process:** Identify Stakeholders
- **What to do:** Identify all people or organizations affected by the project and document their interests, involvement, and impact
- **Tools/Techniques:**
  - Stakeholder analysis
  - Expert judgment
  - Power/interest grid
- **Inputs:**
  - Project charter
  - Business documents
  - Agreements
  - Enterprise environmental factors
  - Organizational process assets
- **Outputs:**
  - Stakeholder register (basic information: names, positions, roles, contact info)
  - Stakeholder engagement assessment matrix

## **PLANNING PROCESS GROUP**

### **Project Scope Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Scope Management
  - **What to do:** Create scope management plan that documents how scope will be defined, validated, and controlled
  - **Tools:** Expert judgment, meetings, analytical techniques
  - **Outputs:** Scope management plan, requirements management plan

- **Process 2:** Collect Requirements
  - **What to do:** Determine, document, and manage stakeholder needs and requirements
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Interviews (formal/informal, one-to-one)
    - Focus groups (prequalified stakeholders and subject matter experts)
    - Group creativity techniques (brainstorming, benchmarking)
    - Questionnaires and surveys (collect feedback from large groups)
    - Observation (direct viewing of individuals in their environment)
    - Prototyping (build simpler product first)
  - **Outputs:**
    - Requirements documentation
    - Requirements traceability matrix (RTM)

- **Process 3:** Define Scope
  - **What to do:** Develop detailed description of the project and product
  - **Key consideration:** Solutions must consider the system as a whole
  - **Outputs:** Project scope statement (describes deliverables, boundary, included/excluded work)

- **Process 4:** Create WBS
  - **What to do:** Subdivide project deliverables into smaller, manageable components
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Decomposition (subdividing deliverables)
    - WBS templates
    - Top-down or bottom-up approach
    - Analogy approach (review similar projects)
  - **Outputs:**
    - Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
    - WBS dictionary
    - Scope baseline

### **Project Time Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Schedule Management
  - **What to do:** Establish policies, procedures, and documentation for planning, developing, managing, executing, and controlling the project schedule
  - **Outputs:** Schedule management plan

- **Process 2:** Define Activities
  - **What to do:** Identify specific actions to be performed to produce project deliverables
  - **Outputs:**
    - Activity list (tasks from WBS)
    - Activity attributes (predecessors, successors, constraints)
    - Milestone list (significant events with zero duration)

- **Process 3:** Sequence Activities
  - **What to do:** Identify and document relationships among project activities
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Precedence Diagramming Method (PDM)
    - Arrow Diagramming Method (ADM)
    - Dependency determination (mandatory, discretionary, external)
  - **Outputs:** Project schedule network diagrams

- **Process 4:** Estimate Activity Durations
  - **What to do:** Estimate number of work periods needed to complete individual activities
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Three-point estimates (optimistic, most likely, pessimistic)
    - Expert judgment
    - Analogous estimating
    - Parametric estimating
    - Bottom-up estimating
  - **Outputs:** Duration estimates, basis of estimates

- **Process 5:** Develop Schedule
  - **What to do:** Analyze activity sequences, durations, resource requirements to create project schedule
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Critical Path Method (CPM)
    - Critical Chain Scheduling
    - Resource leveling
    - Gantt charts
    - Program Evaluation and Review Technique (PERT)
  - **Outputs:** Schedule baseline, project schedule

### **Project Cost Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Cost Management
  - **What to do:** Establish policies, procedures, and documentation for planning, managing, expending, and controlling project costs
  - **Outputs:** Cost management plan

- **Process 2:** Estimate Costs
  - **What to do:** Develop approximation of monetary resources needed to complete project activities
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Analogous estimating
    - Parametric estimating
    - Bottom-up estimating
    - Three-point estimates
    - Reserve analysis
  - **Types of costs considered:**
    - Tangible vs. intangible
    - Direct vs. indirect
    - Sunk costs
    - Learning curve theory
  - **Outputs:** Activity cost estimates, basis of estimates

- **Process 3:** Determine Budget
  - **What to do:** Aggregate estimated costs of individual activities to establish authorized cost baseline
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Cost aggregation
    - Reserve analysis
    - Funding limit reconciliation
  - **Outputs:** Cost baseline, project funding requirements

### **Project Quality Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Quality Management
  - **What to do:** Identify quality requirements and/or standards and document how project will demonstrate compliance
  - **Key concepts:**
    - Quality as "degree to which inherent characteristics fulfill requirements" (ISO)
    - Conformance to requirements
    - Fitness for use
    - Customer satisfaction
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Cost-benefit analysis
    - Cost of quality (prevention, appraisal, failure costs)
    - Benchmarking
    - Design of experiments
    - Statistical sampling
  - **Outputs:** Quality management plan, quality metrics, quality checklists

### **Project Resource Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Resource Management
  - **What to do:** Define how to estimate, acquire, manage, and use physical and team resources
  - **Importance:** People determine success and failure of organizations and projects
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Organizational charts and position descriptions
    - Responsibility Assignment Matrix (RAM)
    - RACI chart (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)
  - **Outputs:** Resource management plan, team charter, project organization charts

- **Process 2:** Estimate Activity Resources
  - **What to do:** Estimate type and quantities of resources required for each activity
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Expert judgment
    - Bottom-up estimating
    - Analogous estimating
    - Parametric estimating
  - **Outputs:** Resource requirements, resource breakdown structure

### **Project Communications Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Communications Management
  - **What to do:** Develop appropriate approach and plan for project communications based on stakeholder information needs
  - **Key fact:** Project managers spend up to 90% of time communicating
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Communication requirements analysis
    - Communication technology selection
    - Communication models
    - Communication methods (interactive, push, pull)
    - Communication styles matrix (considering introvert/extrovert, thinking/feeling preferences)
  - **Formulas:**
    - Number of communication channels = n(n-1)/2 where n = number of people
  - **Outputs:** Communications management plan

### **Project Risk Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Risk Management
  - **What to do:** Define how to conduct risk management activities
  - **Key definition:** Risk = uncertainty that can have negative or positive effect
  - **Tools/Techniques:** Analytical techniques, expert judgment, meetings
  - **Outputs:** Risk management plan

- **Process 2:** Identify Risks
  - **What to do:** Determine which risks may affect project and document their characteristics
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Brainstorming
    - Delphi technique
    - Interviewing
    - SWOT analysis
    - Checklist analysis
    - Assumption analysis
    - Diagramming techniques
  - **Outputs:** Risk register (list of identified risks, potential responses, root causes)

- **Process 3:** Perform Qualitative Risk Analysis
  - **What to do:** Prioritize risks for further analysis by assessing probability and impact
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Risk probability and impact assessment
    - Probability/impact matrix
    - Risk categorization
    - Risk urgency assessment
  - **Outputs:** Updated risk register

- **Process 4:** Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis
  - **What to do:** Numerically analyze effect of identified risks on project objectives
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Expected Monetary Value (EMV) analysis
    - Decision tree analysis
    - Sensitivity analysis
    - Modeling and simulation (Monte Carlo)
  - **Outputs:** Updated risk register

- **Process 5:** Plan Risk Responses
  - **What to do:** Develop options and actions to enhance opportunities and reduce threats
  - **Strategies for negative risks:**
    - Avoid (eliminate threat)
    - Transfer (shift to third party)
    - Mitigate (reduce probability/impact)
    - Accept (acknowledge)
  - **Strategies for positive risks:**
    - Exploit (ensure opportunity realized)
    - Share (allocate to third party)
    - Enhance (increase probability/impact)
    - Accept
  - **Outputs:** Updated risk register, risk-related contract decisions

### **Project Procurement Management**
- **Process 1:** Plan Procurement Management
  - **What to do:** Document procurement decisions, specify approach, identify potential sellers
  - **Why outsource?**
    - Access skills and technologies
    - Reduce costs
    - Focus on core business
    - Provide flexibility
    - Increase accountability
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Make-or-buy analysis
    - Market research
    - Expert judgment
  - **Contract types:**
    - Fixed price (FFP, FPIF, FPECA)
    - Time and material (T&M)
    - Cost-reimbursable (CPFF, CPAF, CPIF)
  - **Outputs:** Procurement management plan, procurement statement of work, procurement documents

### **Project Stakeholder Management**
- **Process:** Plan Stakeholder Engagement
  - **What to do:** Develop approaches to involve stakeholders based on needs, expectations, interests, and potential impact
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Analytical techniques
    - Expert judgment
    - Meetings
  - **Outputs:** Stakeholder engagement plan

    - Knowledge management tools
    - Information management tools

  - **Outputs:** Lessons learned register, organizational process assets updates

### **Project Quality Management**
- **Process:** Manage Quality (Quality Assurance)

  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Quality audits
    - Process analysis
    - Quality management and control tools
    - Root cause analysis
  - **Outputs:** Quality reports, test and evaluation documents, change requests

### **Project Resource Management**
- **Process 1:** Acquire Resources
  - **What to do:** Obtain team members, facilities, equipment, materials, and other resources
  - **Key challenge:** Right people at right time (competition with other teams)
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Negotiation
    - Pre-assignment
    - Virtual teams
    - Multi-criteria decision analysis
  - **Outputs:** Physical resource assignments, project team assignments, resource calendars

- **Process 2:** Develop Team
  - **What to do:** Improve competencies, team interaction, and overall team environment
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Training (give new knowledge/skill)
    - Coaching (help develop to acquire new knowledge/skill)
    - Team-building activities (physical challenges, psychological preference tools)
    - Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) - 16 personality types
    - Reward and recognition systems
  - **Stages of team development:**
    1. Forming (figuring out roles)
    2. Storming (conflicts as opinions form)
    3. Norming (adjusting work habits to help each other)
    4. Performing (acting as cohesive unit)
    5. Adjourning (dealing with project closure)
  - **Outputs:** Team performance assessments, enterprise environmental factors updates

- **Process 3:** Manage Team
  - **What to do:** Track team member performance, provide feedback, resolve issues, manage changes
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Observation and conversation
    - Project performance appraisals
    - Conflict management techniques:
      1. Confrontation (direct problem-solving)
      2. Compromise (give-and-take)
      3. Smoothing (emphasize agreement)
      4. Forcing (win-lose approach)
      5. Withdrawal (retreat from disagreement)
      6. Collaborating (incorporate different viewpoints)
  - **Five dysfunctions of teams:**
    1. Absence of trust
    2. Fear of conflict
    3. Lack of commitment
    4. Avoidance of accountability
    5. Inattention to results
  - **Outputs:** Change requests, project management plan updates

### **Project Time Management**

    - Variance analysis
  - **Outputs:** Work performance information, schedule forecasts, change requests

### **Project Cost Management**
- **Process:** Control Costs
  - **What to do:** Monitor status; update costs; manage cost baseline changes
  - **Key technique:** Earned Value Management (EVM)
    - **Planned Value (PV):** Authorized budget for work scheduled
    - **Earned Value (EV):** Value of work actually completed
    - **Actual Cost (AC):** Actual cost incurred
    - **Schedule Variance (SV) = EV - PV**
    - **Cost Variance (CV) = EV - AC**
    - **Schedule Performance Index (SPI) = EV/PV**
    - **Cost Performance Index (CPI) = EV/AC**
    - **Estimate at Completion (EAC) = BAC/CPI**
    - **To-Complete Performance Index (TCPI) = (BAC - EV)/(BAC - AC)**
  - **Outputs:** Work performance information, cost forecasts, change requests

### **Project Quality Management**
- **Process:** Control Quality
  - **What to do:** Monitor and record results of quality activities to assess performance
  - **Tools/Techniques:**
    - Checklists
    - Statistical sampling
    - Inspection
    - Control charts (Seven Run Rule: if 7 points in row below/above mean or increasing/decreasing, process needs examination)
    - Pareto diagrams (80-20 rule)
    - Cause-and-effect diagrams (fishbone/Ishikawa)
    - Histograms
    - Scatter diagrams
  - **Outputs:** Quality control measurements, verified deliverables, work performance information

## **CROSS-CUTTING THEMES AND CONCEPTS**

### **Leadership and Management**
- **Leadership styles** (Daniel Goleman):
  1. Visionary
  2. Coaching
  3. Affiliative
  4. Democratic
  5. Pacesetting
  6. Commanding

### **Motivation Theories**
- **Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs:** Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-actualization
- **Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory:**
  - Motivational factors: achievement, recognition, work itself, responsibility, advancement, growth
  - Hygiene factors: salary, supervision, work environment
- **McGregor's Theory X and Y:**
  - Theory X: Workers dislike work, need coercion
  - Theory Y: Work is natural, enjoy satisfaction
  - Theory Z (Ouchi): Trust, quality, collective decision making

### **Influence and Power**
- **Types of power:**
  - Legitimate (by position)
  - Reward (award bonuses)
  - Expert (respected for expertise)
  - Referent (admiration, loyalty)
- **Ways to influence projects:**
  - Authority, assignment, budget, promotion, money, penalty, work challenge, expertise, friendship
- **Covey's 7 Habits for Project Managers:**
  1. Be proactive
  2. Begin with end in mind
  3. Put first things first
  4. Think win/win
  5. Seek first to understand, then be understood
  6. Synergize
  7. Sharpen the saw

### **Emotional Intelligence**
- Knowing and managing own emotions and understanding others' emotions
- 71% of U.S. hiring managers value EI more than IQ

### **Workforce Issues**
- **Work-life balance:** Europeans take 6-7 weeks paid leave vs. 12 days in U.S.
- **Skills gap:** Entry-level workers deficient in written communications (27.8%), writing in English (26.2%), leadership (23.8%)
- **IT workforce challenges:** 68% of IT firms report difficulty finding staff; 58% concerned about quality/quantity of IT talent

### **Best Practices and Common Challenges**
- **Scope creep:** Uncontrolled changes in scope causing extra work
- **Requirements management:** Only 49% have resources to do it properly; 53% fail to use formal validation process
- **Risk management maturity:** Lowest maturity rating of all knowledge areas (2.75-2.93)
- **Schedule conflicts:** Main reason for conflicts, especially in latter stages
- **Cost estimation problems:** Done too quickly, lack of experience, human bias toward underestimation
- **Communication challenges:** Rarely does receiver interpret message exactly as sender intended

### **Tools and Templates**
- **Project management information systems:** Hundreds available, moving toward enterprise systems
- **Templates:** For communications, charters, plans, reports
- **Software tools:** For scheduling, cost control, communication, risk analysis, procurement
